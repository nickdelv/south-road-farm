Sixty acres of Maine countryside, two hand-built barns, and a farmhouse that has been in the family for generations. This is where South Road Farm began — and where it still lives.

The Land

Sixty acres
of quiet.

South Road Farm sits on sixty acres of rolling farmland in Fayette, Maine — bordered by stone walls, open meadow, and old-growth forest. The land has been farmed in some form since the mid-1800s. We inherited the bones of it: the fields, the stone walls, the barn foundations, the view.

What we built on top of that is something we hope feels both honest and considered. Nothing here is for show. Everything has a reason.

"We wanted a place that felt like it had always been here."

The Barns

Built by
hand.

The main barn was rebuilt timber-frame using locally milled pine and reclaimed wood from the original structure. Every joint was cut and fitted on-site. The second barn — smaller, darker, more intimate — was left closer to its original form, with rough-hewn beams and wide board floors that show their age.

Together they offer flexibility: a ceremony space that holds reverence, a reception hall that comes alive, and a pair of structures that photograph differently in every season and every light.

The Farmhouse

Yours for
the weekend.

The farmhouse — four bedrooms, two and a half baths, a wide front porch, and a kitchen that can actually feed people — is included in every booking. It sleeps eight to ten and serves as a home base for the wedding party from Friday afternoon through Sunday morning.

There is something about waking up on your wedding day in the same place where the ceremony will happen. No shuttle, no timeline anxiety, no racing against the clock. Just coffee on the porch and the morning ahead of you.
