South Road Farm  ·  Fayette, Maine

The
Weekend.

From Friday evening welcome drinks to a ceremony under open skies, late-night dancing, and Sunday morning coffee — this is what a weekend here looks like.

Friday

Arrive.
Settle in.

Evening

Welcome
drinks.

The farmhouse fills up. Someone opens a bottle. Welcome drinks happen in the courtyard without an agenda. This is the unhurried part — before the ceremony, before the speeches, before any of it. The weekend begins exactly as it should.

Saturday

The main
event.

The Ceremony

Open sky.
Open heart.

Multiple ceremony sites — an open field with panoramic views in every direction, and a wooded nook for something more intimate. A wooden arch built by hand. Rows of wooden benches. Land that does most of the heavy lifting.

Cocktail Hour

The bar's open.
Golden hour's here.

The ceremony ends and everyone exhales. Live music fills the field. Guests drift between the fire pits, the lawn, and the courtyard. The bar — whatever form it takes — is yours to decide.

Food trucks. Firelight.
The whole scene.

Late Night

The part
nobody forgets.

The barn lights low. String lights out front. Someone swapped their shoes hours ago. The food window is still glowing. The party finds its own pace — because we built the space to let it.

Sunday

Slow down.
Linger a little.

Morning

Coffee.
No rush.

The farmhouse kitchen. Coffee. Whatever's left over from the night before. No rush, no checkout pressure. Sunday morning at South Road Farm is part of every booking — a few last unhurried hours before the weekend releases you back to the world.
