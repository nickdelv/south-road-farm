The Experience

Not a venue.
A gathering.

South Road Farm isn't built for weddings in the traditional sense. No banquet hall, no required caterer, no schedule laminated to the wall. Two restored barns, 66 acres of open sky, a farmhouse that sleeps your people, and three days that are entirely yours.

The energy builds from Friday evening welcome drinks to a ceremony under open skies, late-night dancing, food trucks, and firelight — and eases into Sunday morning with coffee and the best kind of slow. You shape it. We designed the space to let that happen.

Friday Evening

Arrive.
Settle in.

Your people trickle in through the afternoon. The farmhouse fills up. Someone opens a bottle. The bar is set up in the courtyard and welcome drinks happen without an agenda — just the first golden hour of a long weekend taking shape exactly as it should.

Saturday Night

No script.
Just the party.

The ceremony is done. The first dance is done. Now comes the part nobody forgets — a taco truck glowing neon pink, sneakers on the dance floor, music turned all the way up, jackets shed hours ago. Celebrations here find their own rhythm. We built the space to let that happen.

Sunday Morning

Slow down.
Linger a little.

The farmhouse kitchen. Coffee. Whatever's left over from the night before. No rush, no checkout pressure. Sunday morning at South Road Farm is part of every booking — a few last unhurried hours before the weekend releases you back to the world.

Our Story

It started with
a wedding arch.

In 2022, Nick asked his brother-in-law Dave — a lifelong carpenter — to build an arch for a small wedding. That arch now stands on the ceremony lawn and quietly marks the beginning of everything you see here. This place was built by hand because it couldn't have been built any other way.
